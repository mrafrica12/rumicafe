Rooted in Africa

Where every
ritual was born.

Coffee is not an invention. It is a memory. The first cup was poured in Ethiopia, shared between people sitting close, in no hurry to leave. That impulse — to brew something carefully and offer it generously — is what Rumi carries.

Our sourcing runs deep. The beans in your cup come from smallholder farms across East and West Africa, traded directly, roasted lightly, and served in a space that understands where they came from.

This is not origin story as marketing. It is obligation, and pleasure, and pride.

Origin Profiles

The farms behind
every cup.

Ethiopia

Yirgacheffe
Highland

Bright, floral, and tea-like. Grown at 1,800–2,200m. Notes of jasmine, bergamot, and stone fruit — the birthplace of coffee.

Washed Process · Light Roast
Kenya

Nyeri
AA Grade

Bold, complex, and winey. Black currant, tomato, and grapefruit — Kenya AA is among the world's finest. Sourced from small cooperatives.

Washed Process · Medium Roast
Rwanda

Musasa
Cooperative

Sweet, balanced, and clean. Caramel, red apple, and brown sugar. Rwanda's hillside farms produce some of Africa's most consistent lots.

Natural Process · Light-Medium

A handcrafted café experience rooted in African heritage and the culture of slow coffee.
